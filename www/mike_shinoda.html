Michael "Mike" Shinoda

Michael Kenji Shinoda (Panorama City, February 11, 1977) is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, multi-instrumentalist, record producer, and painter, founder of the alternative rock band Linkin Park.

Within Linkin Park, Shinoda is a driving creative force, contributing rapping, songwriting, melodic vocals, keyboards, and rhythm guitar. He is also known for his production work for both Linkin Park and other artists. Beyond music, Shinoda is a talented visual artist and designer, having handled much of the band's album artwork.

After Chester Bennington's passing, Mike Shinoda also embarked on a solo career, releasing the deeply personal album "Post Traumatic" in 2018, which explores his grieving process. Shinoda was ranked 72nd on Hit Parader's list of the "Top 100 Metal Vocalists of All Time," although his primary contributions are in rapping and composition.
